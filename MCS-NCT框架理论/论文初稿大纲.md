#### **Abstract**（约 250 词）

```
We propose the Multi-Constraint Satisfaction (MCS) theory of consciousness, 
which posits that conscious states emerge from the simultaneous satisfaction 
of six distinct constraints: sensory coherence, temporal continuity, 
self-consistency, action feasibility, social interpretability, and 
integrated information (Φ). Unlike existing theories that focus on single 
mechanisms, MCS provides a unified framework integrating insights from 
Global Workspace Theory, Integrated Information Theory, STDP learning, 
predictive coding, gamma synchronization, and neuromodulation. We present 
a complete mathematical formalization and a PyTorch implementation that 
computes consciousness levels from multi-modal sensory inputs. Experiments 
demonstrate that MCS can distinguish different conscious states and provide 
interpretable constraint violation patterns. The framework offers a 
computational bridge between philosophical theories of consciousness and 
practical AI systems, with potential applications in consciousness disorder 
diagnosis, AI safety, and cognitive robotics.

Keywords: consciousness, constraint satisfaction, computational model, 
integrated information, global workspace theory
```

```
V₁(S,t) = α_t·max(0,|Δt|-δ_t)/δ_t
```
