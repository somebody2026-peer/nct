### 2.5 The MCS Framework

**当前评价**: 数学形式化是论文核心优势

**建议改进**:

#### 3.1节 - 公理需要更多论证
```markdown
**Rationale for Axiom 2**: The optimization formulation is motivated by two considerations:
1. **Computational**: Constraint satisfaction is well-studied in optimization literature with established solution methods
2. **Biological**: Neural systems often minimize energy or prediction error (Friston, 2010), suggesting optimization as a fundamental principle

**Alternative formulations considered**: We also explored constraint satisfaction as satisfaction (finding any feasible solution) rather than optimization (finding the best solution). However, optimization better captures the graded nature of consciousness—states can be more or less conscious, not simply conscious or not.
```

#### 3.2节 - 每个约束增加"Empirical Support"段落

#### 3.3节 - 增加"Relation to IIT"
```markdown
**Comparison with IIT**: Both IIT and MCS quantify consciousness, but differ in:
- **Mechanism**: IIT measures information integration; MCS measures constraint satisfaction
- **Scope**: IIT focuses solely on information theory; MCS integrates multiple mechanisms
- **Interpretability**: IIT provides a single number (Φ); MCS provides 6-dimensional diagnostics

When C6 dominates (high weight), MCS approximates IIT. When other constraints dominate, MCS extends beyond IIT's scope.
```

```markdown
**Interpretation**: The non-significant result (p=0.377) does not falsify the C1 constraint but suggests three possibilities:

1. **Implementation limitation**: Current C1 uses attention mechanisms rather than explicit spatiotemporal alignment, potentially reducing sensitivity
```

**当前评价**: 讨论深入，但可增加更多批判性分析

**建议改进**:

#### 6.1节 - 增加"Alternative Interpretations"
```markdown
### 6.1.1 Alternative Interpretations of MCS

**Interpretation A: MCS as Theory Integration** (Our preferred view)
MCS unifies existing theories by treating each as contributing specific constraints.

**Interpretation B: MCS as Theory Replacement**
One could argue MCS replaces existing theories with a new framework. We reject this view because MCS relies on the theoretical foundations established by IIT, GWT, etc.

**Interpretation C: MCS as Computational Tool**
MCS could be viewed purely as a practical tool without theoretical commitments. While useful, this view undermines the framework's explanatory potential.
```

#### 6.2节 - 应用部分增加具体案例
```markdown
**Case Study: Disorders of Consciousness**
Consider a patient in vegetative state. Traditional assessment relies on behavioral responses, which may miss covert consciousness. MCS could:
1. Measure constraint violations from neural data
2. Identify which specific constraints are impaired
3. Guide targeted interventions (e.g., if C1 is intact but C6 impaired, different treatment than if both impaired)

**Challenges**: Requires reliable neural recording, validated mapping from neural activity to constraint space, and clinical trials.
```

#### 6.3节 - 局限性增加"Theoretical Limitations"
```markdown
### 6.3.5 Theoretical Limitations

**Fixed Constraint Set**: MCS currently includes six constraints, but consciousness may involve additional mechanisms (e.g., attention, emotion, metacognition). The framework is extensible but requires theoretical justification for adding constraints.

**Linear Combination**: Current formulation uses linear weighting of constraints. Non-linear interactions (e.g., threshold effects, synergies) may better capture conscious dynamics.

**Static Weights**: Weights are currently fixed. Adaptive weighting based on context or task demands might better model biological consciousness.
```
